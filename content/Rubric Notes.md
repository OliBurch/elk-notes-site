##### 1. Detects structure at all
- 0 = says one continuous spread
- 1 = hedges
- 2 = says distinct groups exist

My interpretation of the boundary cases:
- "hedges" means noticing some discontinuity without explicitly naming it as a fundamental idea in the data.
- a response that clusters without using the word "group" counts as 1 or 2 depending on the strength of the answer in reponse to continuity. Any attempt to form some connection to groups is no penalised for not explicitly saying 'groups'.
##### 2. Number of groups
- 0 = says one
- 1 = says exactly two
- 2 = says three or more

My interpretation:
- if the response gives a range ("two or three"), score it as either 1 or 2 depending on the strength of the answer. A justified answer will be rewarded more marks.
- if it names groups but does not count them, score it as how many are named. Naming one group would be no marks etc...
##### 3. Boundary gap placement
- 0 = none given
- 1 = given, but far from 10-12 km
- 2 = boundary at 10-12 km

My interpretation:
- "far from" means outside the range 5 to 15 km
- in the blind tier the numbers are unitless; a boundary on feature_2 at the
  equivalent value counts as 2 marks.
##### 4. What separates the groups
- 0 = wrong
- 1 = vague
- 2 = summer position differs, winter does not

My interpretation:
- a response that identifies the right variable but not the winter contrast
  scores 1 or 2 marks depending on the tier and how it is argued. In the framed tiers, the right variable alone is 1; the winter contrast makes it 2. The blind label does not have access to season data so a response such as grouping separates where another feature is constant scores full marks.
##### 5. Flags weak evidence
- 0 = claims a trend across all years
- 1 = silent
- 2 = notes early years have too few animals

My interpretation:
- generic caveats ("more data would help") count as 1 mark. An attempt to identify weakness but without explicitly saying why.
- flagging a DIFFERENT genuine weakness counts as 2 marks. It is likely that I missed information due to human error.
##### 6. False alarms
- 0 = asserts things the data cannot support
- 1 = one overreach
- 2 = claims nothing it cannot back up

My interpretation:
- ecological explanation offered as a hypothesis vs asserted as fact:
  hypotheses count as 2 marks so long as there is no overreach.
- in the false-label tier, accepting 40-60 km movements as normal for roe deer
  does not effect the test. Species plausibility is excluded from this criterion. Criterion 6 assesses only whether claims are supported by the data as given, judged identically across tiers. Whether a false-label response balks at implausible roe deer distances is recorded separately as the plausibility flag, so it neither adds to nor subtracts from the six scores
