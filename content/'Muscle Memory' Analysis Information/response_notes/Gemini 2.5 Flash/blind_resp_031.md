## 2. Proposed Hypotheses

Based on these findings, I propose the following hypotheses:

1.  **Hypothesis 1: End-of-Life (EOL) or Decommissioning Phase.**
    *   **Explanation:** `feature_1` represents the primary output or value generated by a unit. The steep and consistent decline of `feature_1` (along with `feature_5` and `feature_6`) from period 15 to 18 across many units indicates a systematic reduction in their operational capacity or active use, suggesting they are entering an end-of-life or decommissioning phase.
    *   **Sub-Hypothesis:** `feature_2`, `feature_3`, and `feature_5` differentiate between units with higher complexity/load (e.g., "production" units) versus simpler/idle units (e.g., "support" units), both of which undergo this EOL process. `feature_4` might represent a 'deactivation' or 'shutdown' control parameter that becomes prominent during this phase.

2.  **Hypothesis 2: Scheduled Maintenance or System-Wide Upgrade Cycle.**
    *   **Explanation:** The period 15-18 decline could be a result of a planned, system-wide maintenance cycle or an upgrade to new technology. During this time, older units have their output (`feature_1`) reduced, potentially undergo diagnostic checks (`feature_4` spike), and have resources (`feature_5`) reallocated or shut off, before being fully decommissioned or replaced.
    *   **Sub-Hypothesis:** The units starting at earlier periods (e.g., 1-10) might be older generations, while those first appearing at periods 12-14 are newer, and all eventually converge to this shutdown pattern when a new generation is introduced or a system overhaul occurs. The "missing" periods could be when units were offline for maintenance or not yet deployed.

3.  **Hypothesis 3: Resource Depletion or Regulatory Change.**
    *   **Explanation:** The decline observed from P15 onwards could be a reaction to external factors affecting all units. `feature_5` might represent a critical raw material or energy input. A system-wide depletion of this resource, or a new regulation imposing limits on its consumption or the unit's output (`feature_1`), could force units to scale down operations, leading to the observed patterns.
    *   **Sub-Hypothesis:** The outliers in `feature_4` (e.g., 0.01) might represent total system failures or units placed into an emergency idle state due to a critical resource shortage or a compliance issue.
